Session Proposal & Call for Contributions

Achieving Adoption of Solid at Scale

Share your idea Email the organiser
Session lead
Jesse Wright (University of Oxford · Open Data Institute)
Duration
90 minutes · SoSy 2026 main programme

Abstract

Solid was born out of MIT in 2015. A decade later the technology is still not deployed at scale. Solid is foundational, but deployment demands additional technical and governance layers: shared ontologies that hold up in production, signals of trust for applications and storage, and pathways to reputable services. This session is an open call for the toughest adoption challenges and the solutions—Solid native or borrowed from adjacent ecosystems—that can unblock real deployments.

We welcome gaps, solution proposals, and references to existing tooling (dataspaces, trust frameworks, policy playbooks, etc.).

What We Are Looking For

  • Open questions without answers—but with clear impact.
  • Open questions paired with proposed solutions.
  • Case studies where another ecosystem solved a similar gap.
  • Trusted storage, assurance, ontology governance, or compliance practices.

Not in Scope

Business model exploration has a dedicated SoSy session. Keep the focus on the technical, assurance, and governance layers that enable organisations to deploy Solid responsibly at scale.
